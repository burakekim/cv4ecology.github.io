Summer School on Computer Vision Methods for Ecology

Caltech Resnick Sustainability Institute

A three-week intensive summer school teaching Computer Vision Methods for Ecology, hosted at Caltech by the Resnick Sustainability Institute in partnership with the Caltech AI4Science Initiative and NASA JPL. Co-sponsored by Microsoft AI for Earth and Amazon AWS.

Our Mission

Teach Applied CV as a tool for Ecological Research

Provide instruction and tools for an accessible introduction to applied Computer Vision, with the contextual framing of ecological research needs.

Empower ecologists to build their own CV-based systems

Democratize Computer Vision and increase the accessibility and scalability of computer-vision-based solutions to natural world questions

Grow the interdisciplinary CV for Ecology community

Provide a central hub for the exchange of ideas and best practices, where expert ecologists and expert computer vision scientists can work together to face real-world challenges in conservation and sustainability

Provide access to computational resources

Cloud credits to store data, collect annotations, train models, and host solutions are provided by Microsoft AI for Earth and Amazon AWS. Each student will be awarded $2.5K in credits to help take their project from research idea to reality.
